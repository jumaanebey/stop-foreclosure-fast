Your Foreclosure Questions Answered

No judgment, just honest answers and real solutions from people who care.

My auction is in 3 days. Is it too late?

It's not too late! We've stopped foreclosures the day before auction. The key is acting NOW. Call us immediately at (949) 565-5285. We can file emergency paperwork, negotiate with your lender, or arrange a quick sale. Every hour counts at this stage.

Can you really stop a foreclosure in 7 days?

Yes, through several legal methods: filing for bankruptcy (immediate automatic stay), requesting emergency loan modification, arranging a short sale, or finding a cash buyer. The exact strategy depends on your situation, which is why we need to evaluate your case immediately.

What if I just ignore the notices?

Please don't. Ignoring the notices won't make this go away. The bank will proceed with the sale, you'll be evicted (often by sheriff), and you'll lose any equity in your home. Even if you're overwhelmed, one phone call to us can change everything. We handle the stressful parts for you.

I got a Notice of Default. How much time do I have?

In California, after a Notice of Default, you typically have 90 days before a Notice of Sale is recorded, then another 21 days before auction. But don't wait - the sooner we start, the more options you'll have and the better outcome we can achieve.

What happens after I contact you?

Within 30 minutes, we'll call you for a private, no-pressure conversation. We'll review your situation, explain all your options (keep the home, sell it, or walk away), and create an action plan. If you decide to work with us, we immediately start protecting your interests.

Will I have to leave my home immediately?

No! Our first goal is always to help you stay if that's what you want. If staying isn't possible, we'll ensure you have time to relocate with dignity. Even in a sale, you typically have 30-60 days to move, and we can sometimes negotiate cash for relocation.

What documents do I need?

Initially, just basic information about your loan and the notices you've received. We'll help you gather everything else. Don't let missing paperwork stop you from calling - we can work with whatever you have.

Can you negotiate with my bank?

Yes! We negotiate with lenders daily. We speak their language, know what they'll accept, and often achieve solutions homeowners couldn't get on their own: loan modifications, payment plans, principal reductions, or short sale approvals.

How much will your help cost me?

Our consultation is completely FREE. If we help you sell your home, our commission comes from the sale proceeds - you don't pay anything out of pocket. If we help you keep your home through loan modification, we'll discuss any costs upfront. We never ask for money without explaining exactly what it's for.

Will I owe money after foreclosure?

Possibly. If your home sells for less than you owe, the bank might pursue a deficiency judgment. However, we often negotiate to eliminate this debt, or structure sales that protect you from owing anything. Every situation is different, which is why professional help matters.

What if my home is worth less than I owe?

This is called being "underwater," and we have solutions. A short sale lets you sell for less than owed (we negotiate bank approval). Sometimes loan modification can reduce principal. Even if you walk away, we can minimize the financial damage.

Can I get any money from my home's equity?

If you have equity, YES! This is crucial - in foreclosure, the bank takes everything. But if we help you sell quickly, you keep your equity minus selling costs. We've helped clients walk away with tens of thousands instead of nothing.

I'm embarrassed about my situation. Will you judge me?

Never. Life happens - job loss, medical bills, divorce, business failure. We've seen it all and we're not here to judge. You're taking a brave step by seeking help. We treat every client with respect and dignity, focusing on solutions, not blame.

Will my neighbors know?

We handle everything discreetly. While foreclosure notices are public record, we can often resolve things before it becomes neighborhood knowledge. If we need to sell, we market professionally without "distressed sale" signs that embarrass you.

How will this affect my credit?

Foreclosure severely damages credit (200-300 point drop, 7 years on report). But alternatives like short sale or loan modification have less impact. We'll explain how each option affects your credit and help you choose the path that preserves your financial future.

Can I trust you? There are so many scams.

You're right to be cautious. We're licensed California real estate professionals (DRE #02076038). We never ask for upfront fees, never tell you to stop talking to your lender, and never promise unrealistic outcomes. Check our license, read our reviews, and trust your instincts.

What if I just want this nightmare to end?

We understand. This is exhausting and scary. That's why we're here - to take the burden off your shoulders. We handle the calls, paperwork, negotiations, and stress. You just make the decisions. Many clients tell us their biggest relief was simply not facing this alone anymore.
