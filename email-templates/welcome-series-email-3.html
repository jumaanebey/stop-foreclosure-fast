When you're facing foreclosure, it can feel like you have no options. But the truth is, you likely have more options than you realize.

Here's a straightforward breakdown of the most common solutions:

Option 1: Loan Modification

What it is: Your lender changes your loan terms to make payments affordable.

Best for: Homeowners who want to keep their home and can afford modified payments.

Timeline needed: 30-90 days minimum

Option 2: Sell Your Home

What it is: Sell before foreclosure completes, often at or near market value.

Best for: Homeowners with equity who are ready to move on.

Timeline needed: 30-60 days (faster with cash buyers)

Option 3: Short Sale

What it is: Lender agrees to accept less than owed. You sell, they forgive the difference.

Best for: Homeowners who owe more than their home is worth.

Timeline needed: 60-120 days

Option 4: Deed in Lieu

What it is: You transfer the property to your lender to avoid foreclosure on your record.

Best for: Homeowners who can't sell and want to minimize credit damage.

Timeline needed: 30-60 days

Option 5: Forbearance Agreement

What it is: Temporary pause or reduction in payments while you recover financially.

Best for: Homeowners with temporary hardship (job loss, medical issue).

Timeline needed: 14-30 days

The right option depends on YOUR situation.
Your timeline, equity, income, and goals all matter.
