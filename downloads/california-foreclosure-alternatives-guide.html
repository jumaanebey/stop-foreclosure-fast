üí∞ Option 2: Cash Sale to Investor

What It Is:

Selling your home quickly to a cash buyer or real estate investor who can close in days rather than months.

‚úÖ PROS

  • Close in 7-14 days
  • No repairs needed
  • No realtor commissions
  • Immediate foreclosure stop
  • May get cash at closing

‚ùå CONS

  • Below market value offers
  • Must vacate home
  • Need to find new housing
  • Lose future appreciation

Homeowners who need to exit quickly or owe more than their home is worth in the current market.

ü§ù Option 3: Short Sale

What It Is:

Selling your home for less than what you owe with lender approval. The lender agrees to accept the sale proceeds as payment in full.

‚úÖ PROS

  • Less credit damage than foreclosure
  • May get relocation assistance
  • Avoid deficiency judgment (in CA)
  • Control over sale process

‚ùå CONS

  • Takes 3-6 months
  • Requires lender approval
  • Still credit impact
  • Complex paperwork
  • May have tax implications

Best For:

Homeowners who are underwater on their mortgage but want some control over the sale process.

üìã Option 4: Deed in Lieu of Foreclosure

What It Is:

Voluntarily transferring ownership of your property to the lender to satisfy your mortgage debt.

‚úÖ PROS

  • Avoid foreclosure proceedings
  • Less credit damage
  • May qualify for "cash for keys"
  • Faster than foreclosure

‚ùå CONS

  • Lose all equity
  • Must vacate
  • Lender must agree
  • Still affects credit

Best For:

Homeowners who are significantly underwater and want the cleanest exit possible.

‚öñÔ∏è Option 5: Chapter 13 Bankruptcy

What It Is:

Filing bankruptcy to create a court-supervised payment plan that allows you to catch up on missed mortgage payments over 3-5 years.

‚úÖ PROS

  • Immediate automatic stay
  • Keep your home
  • Structured payment plan
  • May strip second mortgages
  • Legal protection

‚ùå CONS

  • Significant credit impact
  • Court supervision for 3-5 years
  • Attorney fees required
  • Must have steady income
  • Public record

Best For:

Homeowners with steady income who can make current payments plus catch up on arrears over time.

üí° Important California Considerations

  • Non-Recourse State: First mortgages on primary residences are typically non-recourse
  • Homeowner Bill of Rights: Provides extra protections during foreclosure process
  • Right to Reinstate: Can cure default until 5 business days before auction
  • Dual Tracking Prohibited: Lenders can't foreclose while reviewing modifications
  • Tenant Protections: If you're renting, tenants have rights during foreclosure
