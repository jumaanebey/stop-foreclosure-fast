Section OneWhat Banks Don't Tell You

Banks lose $50,000 to $100,000 on every foreclosure. That's your leverage. The Hidden Truth

The foreclosure system is designed to seem inevitable. It's not. Banks have loss mitigation departments for one reason: foreclosure is expensive for them too. When you understand this, you realize you're not begging for help—you're offering a solution that benefits both parties.

The California Timeline

Day 1–30

Grace Period

Missed payment. Late fees apply. Easiest time to resolve.

Day 30–120

Pre-Foreclosure

Lender must contact you. No Notice of Default yet.

Day 120+

Notice of Default

Official start. 90 days to reinstate.

Day 210+

Trustee Sale Notice

Auction scheduled. Still time to act.

Key Insight

You have roughly 200 days from first missed payment to auction. More time than you think—but each day reduces your options.

Section TwoThe 72-Hour Action Plan

Day One

Open all mail from your lender. Find your loan number. Calculate what you owe. Check your home's value online.

Day Two

Call Loss Mitigation. Request all options in writing. Ask about forbearance and modification. Document everything.

Day Three

Research California Mortgage Relief. Contact a HUD counselor. Get a professional valuation. Evaluate your best path.

Warning

Don't ignore the situation hoping it resolves itself. The bank will proceed with or without your participation.

Section ThreeScripts for Your Lender

Opening the Conversation

"My name is [name], loan number [number]. I've fallen behind due to [hardship]. I want to resolve this and keep my home. Can you transfer me to Loss Mitigation?"

Requesting Forbearance

"I'm experiencing temporary difficulty and expect to recover within [timeframe]. I'd like to request forbearance—a pause or reduction in payments—while I stabilize. What documentation do you need?"

Asserting Your Rights

"Under the California Homeowner Bill of Rights, I'm entitled to a single point of contact and complete review before any foreclosure action. Can you confirm this and provide your direct contact?"

Section FourCalifornia Legal Rights

Right What It Means
Single Point of Contact One person handles your case. No more runaround.
Dual Tracking Ban No foreclosure while your modification is under review.
120-Day Wait No Notice of Default until 120 days after first missed payment.
Written Denial If denied, bank must explain why and how to appeal.

California Mortgage Relief

Up to $80,000 in assistance. No repayment required. Apply at CaMortgageRelief.org

Section FiveFive Paths Forward

Home worth $600K, you owe $400K. Foreclosure: you get nothing. Sell: you keep $175K+. The Equity Calculation
1

Loan Modification

Adjust terms to lower payments. Reduced rate, extended term, or principal reduction.

2

Forbearance

Pause payments temporarily. Catch up later. Best for short-term hardship.

3

Sell Before Auction

Keep your equity. Cash buyers can close in 7 days if needed.

4

Refinance

New loan, new terms. Can cash out equity to clear other debts.

5

Reverse Mortgage (62+)

Eliminate monthly payments. Stay in your home for life.

Section SixMistakes to Avoid

Ignoring the Problem

Those letters contain deadlines. The bank will proceed without you.

Waiting for a Miracle

Hope isn't a strategy. Act now with what you have.

Not Opening Mail

Legal deadlines are inside. Missing them means losing rights.

Falling for Scams

Red flags: upfront fees, guarantees, requests to sign over your deed.

Giving Up Early

Homes have been saved days before auction. Fight until the end.

Going It Alone

Banks have lawyers. You deserve someone in your corner too.
