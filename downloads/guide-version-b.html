272-HOUR EMERGENCY ACTION PLAN

DAY 1: ASSESS THE SITUATION

  • Open ALL mail from your lender — deadlines are inside
  • Find your loan number and servicer contact
  • Calculate missed payments and total owed
  • Check home value (Zillow/Redfin)
Don't Do This Do NOT ignore notices hoping they'll disappear.

The bank will proceed without you. Every day of inaction shrinks your options.

DAY 2: MAKE CONTACT

Call the Loss Mitigation department (NOT regular customer service):

  1. Request all hardship options in writing
  2. Ask about forbearance (payment pause)
  3. Ask about loan modification
  4. Document EVERYTHING — names, dates, what was said

DAY 3: EXPLORE ALL OPTIONS

  • Research California Mortgage Relief Program
  • Get professional home valuation
  • Contact HUD-approved counselor (free)
  • Evaluate quick sale to protect equity

3WORD-FOR-WORD LENDER SCRIPTS

What you say determines what options you get. Use these exact scripts:

SCRIPT 1: OPENING CALL

"Hi, my name is [NAME] and my loan number is [NUMBER]."

"I've fallen behind on my mortgage due to [job loss / medical issue / divorce]."

"I want to keep my home. Can you transfer me to Loss Mitigation to discuss my options?"

SCRIPT 2: REQUESTING FORBEARANCE

"I'm experiencing a temporary hardship due to [reason]. I expect to recover within [timeframe]."

"I'm requesting forbearance to pause or reduce my payments for 3-6 months."

"I understand I'll still owe these payments. What documentation do you need?"

SCRIPT 3: ASSERT YOUR RIGHTS

"Under the California Homeowner Bill of Rights, I have the right to a single point of contact and complete review before foreclosure proceeds."

"Can you confirm my application will be reviewed before any sale moves forward?"

"What is your direct contact information?"

55 WAYS TO SAVE YOUR EQUITY

Example Home worth $600K. You owe $400K. That's $200K equity.

If bank forecloses: you get $0. If you sell: you keep $175K+. Don't let them take what's yours.

1. LOAN MODIFICATION

Lower payments through reduced rate, extended term, or principal reduction.

2. FORBEARANCE

Pause payments, catch up later. Best for temporary hardship.

3. SELL BEFORE AUCTION

Keep your equity. Cash buyers can close in 7 days.

4. REFINANCE

New loan, new terms. Cash out equity to clear debts.

5. REVERSE MORTGAGE (62+)

Eliminate payments. Stay in home for life.
