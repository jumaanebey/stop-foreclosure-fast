1The Truth That Changes Everything

Before we begin, there's something important you need to know ‚Äî something that might surprise you:

üí° Banks actually lose money on foreclosures.

Legal fees, property maintenance, and selling costs add up to $50,000-$100,000 per foreclosure. Your lender would genuinely prefer to work something out with you. This gives you more power than you realize.

The bank has a whole department ‚Äî called Loss Mitigation ‚Äî whose job is to help homeowners like you avoid foreclosure. When you call them, you're not begging. You're offering a solution that works for both of you.

Understanding the Timeline

Knowing where you are helps you feel more in control:

Days 1-30

Grace Period

Missed payment, late fees begin. This is the simplest time to resolve things.

Days 30-120

Outreach Period

Your lender must contact you about options. No foreclosure filing yet.

Days 120+

Notice of Default

Formal process begins. You have 90 days to catch up.

Days 210+

Sale Notice

Auction scheduled. But there's still time to act.

ü§ç Take a breath. You have more time than you might think.

From first missed payment to auction is typically 200+ days. The key is taking that first step, even a small one.

2Your First 72 Hours

Let's break this down into manageable steps. You don't have to do everything at once.

Day 1: Gather & Breathe

  • Open the mail from your lender (we know it's hard, but it helps)
  • Find your loan number ‚Äî it's on any statement
  • Jot down how many payments you've missed
  • Look up your home's approximate value online
üåü A gentle reminder

Ignoring the situation won't make it go away, but facing it ‚Äî even in small steps ‚Äî starts to make things better. You're already doing that by reading this.

Day 2: Make One Call

Call your lender and ask for the Loss Mitigation department. That's it. Just one call. Here's what to ask:

  1. "What options do I have?" (forbearance, modification, etc.)
  2. "Can you send me information in writing?"
  3. "What documents do you need from me?"

Day 3: Explore Support

  • Look into California Mortgage Relief Program
  • Consider calling a HUD-approved counselor (it's free)
  • Think about whether keeping or selling makes more sense for you

3Talking to Your Lender

Phone calls with banks can feel intimidating. Here are some words that might help:

Starting the Conversation

"Hi, my name is [your name] and my loan number is [number]."

"I've been going through some difficulties ‚Äî [briefly share: job loss, health issues, divorce, etc.]"

"I really want to find a solution. Could you tell me what options might be available?"

Asking About Forbearance

"I'm dealing with a temporary situation. I expect things to improve in [timeframe]."

"Would it be possible to pause or reduce my payments for a few months while I get back on my feet?"

"What would you need from me to consider this?"

üíö Remember: The person on the phone is just doing their job.

Being calm and polite goes a long way. Write down their name and what they tell you. You're doing great.

4Your Rights in California

California has strong protections for homeowners. You're entitled to:

Your Right What It Means for You
One Point of Contact The bank must give you one person to work with ‚Äî no more runaround.
Fair Review They can't foreclose while reviewing your application for help.
Time to Act They must wait 120 days after your first missed payment before filing.
Clear Answers If they deny help, they must explain why in writing.

You may qualify for up to $80,000 in assistance ‚Äî and you don't have to pay it back. Visit CaMortgageRelief.org to learn more.

6Things to Watch Out For

A few common pitfalls to avoid:

Waiting Too Long

It's natural to hope things will work out on their own. But acting sooner gives you more options.

Leaving Mail Unopened

We understand it's stressful. But important deadlines are inside those envelopes.

Falling for Scams

Be cautious of anyone asking for large upfront fees or asking you to sign over your deed. Legitimate help doesn't work that way.

Giving Up Hope

Homes have been saved even days before auction. It's not over until it's over.
