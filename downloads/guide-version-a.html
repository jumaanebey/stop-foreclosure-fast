1The Truth Banks Don't Want You to Know

Before we dive into tactics, you need to understand something that changes everything about how you approach this situation:

Key Insight Banks lose $50,000 to $100,000 on every foreclosure.

Legal fees, maintenance, selling costs, and property losses add up fast. Your lender would rather work with you than absorb these costs. This is your leverage.

The bank's Loss Mitigation department exists specifically to avoid foreclosure because it's expensive for them. When you call, you're not begging for mercy — you're offering them a way to lose less money.

2Day 1-3: Emergency Action Plan

If you've received a foreclosure notice or you're behind on payments, here's exactly what to do in the next 72 hours:

Day 1: Stop, Breathe, Gather

  • Open ALL mail from your lender — critical deadlines are inside
  • Find your loan number and servicer contact information
  • Calculate how many payments you've missed and approximate amount owed
  • Check your home's approximate current value (Zillow, Redfin)
Warning Do NOT ignore notices hoping they'll go away. Every day you wait, you lose options. The bank WILL proceed with the sale if you don't respond.

Day 2: Contact Your Lender

Call your lender's Loss Mitigation department (not regular customer service). Your goals:

  1. Request all available hardship options in writing
  2. Ask about forbearance (temporary payment pause)
  3. Inquire about loan modification possibilities
  4. Document the name of everyone you speak with

Day 3: Explore All Options

  • Research California Mortgage Relief Program eligibility
  • Get a professional home valuation to understand your equity
  • Contact a HUD-approved housing counselor (free service)
  • Evaluate if selling quickly could protect your equity

3Exact Scripts for Calling Your Lender

What you say matters. Here are word-for-word scripts that have helped hundreds of homeowners:

Script 1: Initial Loss Mitigation Call

"Hi, my name is [YOUR NAME] and my loan number is [LOAN NUMBER].

I'm calling because I've fallen behind on my mortgage due to [hardship: job loss / medical issue / divorce / reduced income].

I want to keep my home and I'm looking for options to resolve this situation.

Can you please transfer me to your Loss Mitigation department to discuss what programs might be available for me?"

Script 2: Requesting Forbearance

"I'm experiencing a temporary hardship due to [specific reason]. I expect my financial situation to improve within [timeframe] because [reason].

I'm requesting a forbearance agreement that would allow me to pause or reduce my payments for 3-6 months while I get back on my feet.

I understand I'll still owe these payments, but this breathing room would help me avoid foreclosure and pay back everything I owe.

What documentation do you need from me to process this request?"

Script 3: Asserting Your Rights

"Under California law, specifically the California Homeowner Bill of Rights, I have the right to a single point of contact and a complete review of loss mitigation options before foreclosure proceeds.

Can you confirm that my application will be reviewed before any foreclosure sale moves forward?

And can I get your direct contact information so I have a single point of contact as required by law?"

Pro Tips
  • Call in the morning for shorter wait times
  • Write down the date, time, and name of everyone you speak with
  • Follow up every phone call with written confirmation
  • Be persistent but polite — the rep isn't your enemy

4Your Legal Rights in California

California has some of the strongest homeowner protection laws in the country:

The California Homeowner Bill of Rights

👥
Single Point of Contact
One person at the bank handles your case. No more getting bounced around between departments.
🛡
Dual Tracking Ban
If you've submitted a complete modification application, the bank cannot simultaneously proceed with foreclosure.
⏱
120-Day Waiting Period
Lenders cannot file a Notice of Default until 120 days after your first missed payment.
📜
Written Denial Explanation
If denied for modification, the bank must explain why in writing and tell you how to appeal.

California Mortgage Relief Program

  • Up to $80,000 to catch up on past-due mortgage payments
  • Additional assistance for property taxes and HOA dues
  • No repayment required for those who qualify
  • Apply at: CaMortgageRelief.org
