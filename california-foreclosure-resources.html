California Foreclosure Resources

Free and legitimate help for California homeowners facing foreclosure. Government programs, nonprofit assistance, and expert guidance.

Need Help Now?

If you're facing foreclosure in California, these resources are free and legitimate. Be wary of any company charging upfront fees to "save" your home - this is often a scam. All resources listed below are free or government-sponsored.

Government Assistance Programs

State Program

California Mortgage Relief Program

Provides up to $80,000 to eligible California homeowners who experienced financial hardship due to COVID-19. Funds can cover past-due mortgage payments, property taxes, homeowner association fees, and other qualified expenses.

Eligibility: Own and occupy a California home, experienced pandemic-related hardship, income at or below county limits (up to 150% AMI).

Website: CaMortgageRelief.org
Phone: 1-888-840-2594
Federal Agency

HUD Housing Counseling

The U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) funds free housing counseling agencies throughout California. Counselors can help you understand your options, review your finances, communicate with your lender, and submit loan modification applications.

Phone: 1-800-569-4287 (toll-free)
Website: HUD.gov/findacounselor
Federal Program

FHA Loss Mitigation

If you have an FHA loan, you may qualify for special FHA loss mitigation options including FHA-HAMP modification, partial claim, or pre-foreclosure sale. Contact your loan servicer to ask about FHA-specific options.

FHA Resource Center: 1-800-225-5342
Website: HUD.gov FHA Resources
Federal Program

VA Loan Assistance

Veterans with VA-guaranteed loans have access to VA loan technicians who can intervene with your servicer on your behalf. The VA offers several loss mitigation options and can help negotiate with lenders.

VA Loan Hotline: 1-877-827-3702
Website: VA.gov Home Loans

Know Your Rights

California Homeowner Bill of Rights

California law provides important protections for homeowners facing foreclosure:

  • No Dual Tracking: Lenders cannot foreclose while reviewing your loan modification application
  • Single Point of Contact: You're entitled to one person/team handling your case
  • Written Decisions: Lenders must explain in writing why they denied your modification
  • Right to Appeal: You can appeal a denied loan modification

Beware of Foreclosure Scams

Legitimate foreclosure help is free. Watch out for these red flags:

  • Charging upfront fees before providing services
  • Guaranteeing to stop your foreclosure
  • Asking you to sign over your deed
  • Telling you to stop communicating with your lender
  • Asking you to make payments to them instead of your servicer

If you encounter a foreclosure scam, report it to the California Attorney General at 1-800-952-5225.
